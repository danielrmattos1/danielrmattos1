How it works · the honest version

The model, with nothing hidden.

Read this first

No system predicts football with "high accuracy on every match." Outcomes carry real, irreducible randomness, and that's why we watch. The best public and bookmaker models land roughly 55 to 65% on win/draw/loss and far lower on exact scores. GROUNDTRUTH targets that same honest tier and shows you the full probability, never a fake certainty.

1 · Strength → Elo

Each team has a World Football Elo rating. A 100-point gap means the stronger side has about a 64% "win expectancy" at a neutral venue. Home advantage adds +65 Elo for the host side when the neutral toggle is off.

2 · Elo → expected goals

The rating gap dr becomes a goal supremacy: S = dr / 165, capped at ±2.8. With a baseline of T = 2.6 total goals, each side's expected goals (xG) are (T ± S) / 2. The divisor 165 is calibrated so the goal model reproduces the Elo win expectancy almost exactly.

3 · Expected goals → scoreline distribution

Goals are modelled as Poisson processes, giving a probability for every scoreline from 0-0 up to 10-10. A Dixon-Coles correction (ρ = −0.05) nudges the low-scoring cells (0-0, 1-0, 0-1, 1-1) to better match how real football clusters around tight results. Win / draw / loss, over-under, both-teams-to-score and the most likely scorelines are all read straight off this one matrix, fully self-consistent.

4 · Tournament → Monte Carlo

The simulator samples actual goal counts from each team's Poisson, settles knockout draws on a lightly Elo-weighted penalty shootout, and replays the whole bracket 10,000 times to estimate title odds.

Make it sharper

The single biggest upgrade is fresher inputs: update the Elo table after every result. To go further you'd add squad availability (injuries/suspensions), rest days, and recent form. The engine is built so those become extra adjustments to dr and T, which is exactly what the Scout tab does.
